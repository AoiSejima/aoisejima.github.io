  • Invisible

  • December 2021

  • InDesign, Laser Printer, Japanese Stab Binding

  • OBJECTIVE

    Conceptualize a book design from an image.

    INSPIRATION

    An image of an artwork by Do Ho Suh, Home within Home within Home within Home within Home, created in 2013.

    EDITOR'S LETTER

    When you hold this book, I want you to first feel the book. Not read.

  • CONCEPT

    “Intangible”

    I derived this concept from the way Suh uses translucent fabric to build architecture structures to represent the “invisible memory” of daily experiences at home. Understanding his concept as something you cannot physically touch anymore but exist in your mind or heart, I came to the word “intangible.”

    Both invisibility and intangibility correlate with human senses and the surrounding spaces because humans feel the presence of the space, memory, or existence but cannot physically touch or see it.

    "ghostly"
    "ephemeral"

    were the keywords. I utilized translucent paper to display intangibility, and the texts and images form the floating and fade out feelings once they overlap. I also played around with the opacity and spacing of the words and paragraphs to create the fading and dissolving impression.
