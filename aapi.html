  • OBJECTIVE

    "Action Week"

    Contribute to an organization and start an action.

  • PROBLEM

    Crimes targetting Asian women and elderies increased significantly after COVID-19.
    They fear going out side and walking by themselves at night.

  • SOLUTION

    Contributing to the organization called STOP AAPI HATE , aka Asian American Pacific Islander, I designed an application version of their website to make it more accessible for the users.
    Embedding a map that displays which streets are safe to walk would help them feel safer when walking outside. Benefit both the community and the organization, and eventually inform the government / police officers where they need to pay attention to more for environmental justice.

  • TARGET USER

    Immigrants in the USA
    Mostly, Asian women and elderies, who are the target of the crimes and fearful of walking outside.

  • INSPIRATION

    Maintaining the organization's website theme, I utilized their logo and color scheme.
    Opening up an application on your phone is much quicker and easier in an emergency situation to reach out.

  • GOAL

    Benefit both the community and the organization, and eventually inform the government / police officers where they need to pay attention to more, for environmental justice.

New Feature

I embedded the map feature within the app that will display illustrative icons to notify users which streets they should avoid walking.

Blue Flash Light Icon is a sign of patrol taking place.
Red Explanation Triangle Icon is the unprotected/unsanitary streets with broken street lights, overflowing garbage cans, and abandoned buildings. The racism crimes and violence has a tendency to occur on the street or subway that are not protected by the police or environmentally maintained.
Attack (Strick, Knife, Gunshot) Icons symbolize past crimes occured on the streets.

Select Your Language

Target users being immigrants in America, the app is accessible by anyone from any region.

Check Your Surrounding!
